DAPHNE SHUTDOWN TIMEOUT FIX - IMPLEMENTATION COMPLETE
========================================================================

ISSUE FIXED:
  "Application instance took too long to shut down and was killed"

DESCRIPTION:
  When editing files (e.g., ai_views.py), Django's autoreloader restarts
  the ASGI server. Daphne's default 2-second shutdown timeout was too short
  for pending requests to complete gracefully, causing forceful termination
  and warning messages in the console.

SOLUTION:
  Increased Daphne's application_close_timeout from 2 to 5 seconds in
  development mode, allowing pending requests to complete gracefully during
  file reloads.

TECHNICAL EXPLANATION
========================================================================

WHY THE WARNINGS OCCURRED:
  - Daphne default: application_close_timeout = 2 seconds
  - When autoreloader triggers: Old process must shut down
  - If requests don't finish in 2 seconds: Forceful termination occurs
  - Warning message: "took too long to shut down and was killed"

HOW THE FIX WORKS:
  - Increased timeout to 5 seconds in development (settings.py)
  - dev_server.py passes --application-close-timeout 5 to Daphne
  - Pending requests get 5 seconds to finish gracefully
  - No forceful termination needed
  - No warning messages

WHY 5 SECONDS:
  - Enough for most requests to complete during reload
  - Not so long that reload appears to hang
  - Appropriate for development with longer operations
  - Production still uses strict 2-second timeout
